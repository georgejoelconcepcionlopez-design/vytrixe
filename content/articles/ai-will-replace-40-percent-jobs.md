Artificial intelligence continues to evolve rapidly.

Experts predict that automation powered by AI may replace nearly 40 percent of global jobs by the end of the decade. This transition marks one of the most significant shifts in the labor market since the Industrial Revolution.

While many fear widespread unemployment, others argue that new industries and roles will emerge, requiring a combination of technical skill and human empathy that machines cannot currently replicate. 

The focus now shifts to reskilling the workforce and adapting educational systems to meet the demands of the AI-augmented future.
